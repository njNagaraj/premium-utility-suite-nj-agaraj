Mastering Modern Web Branding

In the modern web, a logo is more than just a `favicon.ico`. As Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) become the standard, your website needs to provide a rich set of brand assets for home screens, task switchers, and app stores.

Favicons vs. Touch Icons

Traditional browsers display the 16x16 or 32x32 favicon in the tab. However, when a user booksmarks your site on an iPhone or iPad, iOS looks for the `apple-touch-icon.png` (usually 180x180). Providing high-resolution touch icons ensures your app looks native and premium when saved to a mobile home screen.

Web Manifests & Android Integration

Android devices use the `site.webmanifest` to define how your site behaves when installed as an app. This tool generates the manifest and the required 192x192 and 512x512 icons, allowing your web application to have a professional presence in the Android app drawer.
